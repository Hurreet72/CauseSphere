Famous Zoos

  • Mysore Zoo, Karnataka

    • Mysore Zoo, also known as Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, is home to a variety of exotic and endangered species, playing a key role in wildlife conservation.

  • Delhi Zoo, Delhi

    • Delhi Zoo, or National Zoological Park, houses diverse wildlife, including royal Bengal tigers, Indian elephants, and migratory birds.

  • Pune Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park, Maharashtra

    • Pune's Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park features a snake park, animal rescue center, and a large variety of mammals and reptiles.

  • Arignar Anna Zoological Park, Tamil Nadu

    • Arignar Anna Zoological Park is one of the largest zoos in South Asia, with over 2,500 species, including lions, tigers, and giraffes.

Bird Sanctuaries

  • Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary, Rajasthan

    • Also known as Keoladeo National Park, Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary is a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for migratory birds like Siberian cranes.

  • Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary, Haryana

    • Located near Gurgaon, Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary is a haven for birdwatchers, hosting over 250 bird species.

Wildlife Sanctuaries

  • Jim Corbett National Park, Uttarakhand

    • India's oldest national park, Jim Corbett is famous for its Bengal tiger population and diverse wildlife.

  • Kaziranga National Park, Assam

    • Kaziranga is a UNESCO site, home to the world's largest population of Indian one-horned rhinoceroses.

National Parks

  • Gir National Park, Gujarat

    • Gir National Park is the only place in the world where you can find Asiatic lions in the wild.

  • Bandipur National Park, Karnataka

    • A part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, Bandipur is known for its tigers, elephants, and lush forests.
